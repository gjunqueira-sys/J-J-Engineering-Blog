Decomposition and Synthesis

There are several important results in Discrete signal theory. One of them is the decomposition of signals into Even and Odd signals.

We define Even signals if :

$$Xâ‚‘(-n) = Xâ‚‘(n)$$

Similarly , we define Odd signals if :

$$Xâ‚’(-n) = Xâ‚’(n)$$

Any artibrary real-valued signal can be decomposed into Even and Odd signals:

$$x(n) = xâ‚‘(n) + xâ‚’(n)$$

$$xâ‚‘(n) = \frac{1}{2}[x(n) + x(-n)]$$

$$Xâ‚’(n) = \frac{1}{2}[x(n) - x(-n)]$$
